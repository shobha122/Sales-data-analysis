## Project Overview
This project performs an end-to-end **Retail Sales Business Analysis** using Python.  
The goal is to identify key factors that influence profitability and support data-driven business decisions.

The analysis focuses on three major areas:

1. **Discount Impact on Profitability**
2. **Product Category Performance**
3. **Regional Sales & Profitability Comparison**

---

## Dataset
The dataset used is a publicly available **Superstore-style retail sales dataset**, containing transaction-level information such as:

- Order ID  
- Order Date  
- Customer Segment  
- Category
- Sub-Category  
- Region  
- Sales  
- Discount  
- Profit

## Analysis Breakdown

### 1. Discount Impact Analysis
- Classified discounts into Low, Medium, and High levels  
- Studied how discounting affects profitability  
- Found that excessive discounts often reduce profit margins  

**Key Insight:**  
Higher discount levels can lead to significant profit loss.

---

### 2. Product Performance Analysis
- Compared sales and profit across product categories and sub-categories  
- Identified high-performing and low-performing product groups  

**Key Insight:**  
Certain product categories contribute the majority of overall profitability.

---

### 3. Regional Sales & Profitability Analysis
- Evaluated sales and profit across regions  
- Calculated profit margin differences between regions  

**Key Insight:**  
Sales may appear balanced, but profit margins vary significantly across regions.

---

## Final Business Conclusion
Across discount, product, and regional analysis, profitability was found to depend more on pricing strategy, product mix, and regional efficiency than on sales volume alone.

### Business Recommendations
- Optimize discounting to protect profit margins  
- Focus on high-performing product categories  
- Improve strategies in low-profit regions to maximize business performance
